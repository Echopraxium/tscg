System

The Four-Stroke Engine converts chemical energy (fuel) into continuous mechanical rotation through four sequential phases: Intake → Compression → Power → Exhaust Two crankshaft revolutions (720°) complete one full cycle.

Otto Cycle Thermodynamics

1→2 Isentropic compression — PVγ = const 2→3 Isochoric heat addition — spark ignition 3→4 Isentropic expansion — power stroke 4→1 Isochoric heat rejection — exhaust Ideal efficiency: η = 1 − 1/r(γ−1) where r = compression ratio, γ = 1.4 (air)

TSCG Insight

Primary pattern: m2:Workflow + m2:FeedbackLoop The 4-stroke sequence is a structured ordered workflow (Workflow) that periodically returns to its initial state (FeedbackLoop). Continuous crankshaft rotation emerges from 4 discrete strokes — a canonical m2:Emergence.

GenericConceptCombo — CyclicWorkflow
Workflow ⊗ FeedbackLoop
= (S⊗D⊗F) ⊗ (Process⊗Alignment⊗Homeostasis)
Emergent property: The 4-stroke sequence is both ordered (Workflow — steps cannot be reordered) and cyclic (FeedbackLoop — after Exhaust the system is identical to before Intake). Neither property alone is sufficient.
Transformation Chain
Chemical → Thermal → Mechanical
Three successive m2:Transformation events, each converting energy form. The chain is irreversible: heat cannot spontaneously reconvert to fuel (Second Law of Thermodynamics).
Emergence — Rotation
4 discrete strokes ⇒ continuous rotation
The crankshaft converts 4 non-simultaneous linear impulses into a smooth rotational trajectory. The emergent smoothness requires the flywheel (momentum storage) — a second-order emergence.
