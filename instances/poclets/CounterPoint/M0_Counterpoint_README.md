## 1. System Overview

**Counterpoint** is a compositional system in which N independent melodic voices coexist simultaneously, each pursuing its own melodic trajectory while respecting a shared set of normative constraints — the **Counterpoint Rules**. The system produces **harmonic coherence as an emergent property** of local rule compliance: no single voice "knows" the global harmony; it arises from pairwise interactions governed by the rule set.

Counterpoint is TSCG's first poclet in the **aesthetic domain**. Its significance for the framework is twofold: it validates ASFID applicability beyond natural sciences, and it presents a candidate **m3:Enigma** pattern.

### TSCG Synergy Formula

### Enigma Pattern (m3:Enigma Candidate)

```
Voice Autonomy ⊗ CounterpointRule → Harmonic Coherence
```

Without the Rules: autonomous voices produce chaos. Without autonomy: the Rules produce mere melody. The **CounterpointRule** is not a restriction of autonomy — it is the *enabling condition* of multi-voice coexistence. This ternary mediator structure recurs identically across music, ecology, distributed systems, and urban planning.

## 2. Architecture — 4 Poles

| Pole | Name | Voice Density | Tension Level |
|------|------|--------------|---------------|
| `Pole_Exposition`  | **Exposition**        | Progressive 1→N     | Low               |
| `Pole_Development` | **Development**       | Full N, variable    | Medium            |
| `Pole_Stretto`     | **Stretto**           | Full N, max overlap | High              |
| `Pole_Resolution`  | **Resolution (Coda)** | Full N, converging  | Decreasing → zero |

**Pole 1 — Exposition:** System initialization. All N voices enter successively, each presenting the Subject or its tonal Answer. The polyphonic texture builds incrementally from silence to full N-voice density.

**Pole 2 — Development:** System exploration. Episodes modulate through related keys using motivic fragments from Subject and Countersubject. Middle entries re-present the Subject in new keys.

**Pole 3 — Stretto:** Structural climax. Subject entries overlap — a new voice enters before the previous voice has completed. Polyphonic density reaches its maximum. Not all subjects permit stretto — its presence is evidence of compositional mastery.

**Pole 4 — Resolution (Coda):** System closure. Tonic key reestablished decisively. Final Subject entry over pedal point. Authentic cadence (V⁷ → I) releases all accumulated harmonic tension. In Baroque practice, minor-mode fugues often end on a Picardy third (tonic major).

## 6. Cross-Stylistic Validation

### Canon — Frère Jacques (folk round)
The simplest possible counterpoint: one melody, 4 voices, pure time offset (2-measure delay). All ASFID dimensions present despite maximal simplicity. Zero prior musical knowledge required.

### BWV 578 — 'Little' Fugue in G minor (J.S. Bach) ← **Simulation Primary Reference**
4 voices, G minor, clear and pedagogically transparent structure. All 4 structural poles cleanly identifiable. Bach's most accessible complete fugue.

### BWV 565 — Toccata and Fugue in D minor (J.S. Bach)
The most iconic fugue in the repertoire. The D minor Subject (falling diminished seventh + stepwise run) is the most recognizable in fugal history. The counterpoint system at full expressive power.

### Jazz Modal — Miles Davis / Herbie Hancock Quintet (1964–68)
Counterpoint liberated from written score. Each performer improvises independently while sharing the harmonic grid. The ASFID structure is identical to the Baroque fugue; only the medium of the Rules changes: written notation → internalized stylistic vocabulary.
