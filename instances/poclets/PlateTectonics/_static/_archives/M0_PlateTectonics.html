Geophysical Reality

Mantle: Solid but plastic (ductile) - can flow over millions of years Asthenosphere: Viscous layer on which lithospheric plates float (isostasy) Magma: Only present locally at hotspots, ridges, and subduction zones

Plate Flotation

Plates float on the asthenosphere due to isostatic equilibrium - less dense crust floats on denser mantle, like icebergs on water.

Deformation Propagation

Real Time mode: Deformation of a cell propagates to neighboring cells with decreasing intensity. Stress diffuses through the asthenosphere.

Buildings Visualization

Urban structures: Small prismatic blocks on the continental crust represent buildings. Their height varies based on local stress (taller buildings = higher stress).

Planet Mode

Magma visualization: Red/orange glowing areas on the surface represent where magma reaches the surface (mid-ocean ridges, hotspots, subduction zones).
