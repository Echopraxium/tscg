System

A Trophic Pyramid is a layered hierarchical structure in which each level consumes biomass from below and dissipates ~90% of received energy as heat. Lindeman’s 10% rule produces the universal pyramidal biomass shape — StratifiedDissipation.

Lindeman’s 10% Rule (1942)

η = E(n+1) / E(n) ≈ 0.10 Energy: 10 000 → 1 000 → 100 → 10 kJ/m²/yr

5 Trophic Poles

Level 1 Primary Producers — plants, phytoplankton Level 2 Herbivores — insects, ungulates, zooplankton Level 3 Carnivores — foxes, snakes, predatory fish Level 4 Apex Predators — wolves, sharks, eagles ⊥     Decomposers — fungi, bacteria

Environmental Drivers
