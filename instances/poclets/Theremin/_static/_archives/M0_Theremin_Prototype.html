Theremin - Interactive Musical Instrument

Theremin Simulation

The theremin is an electronic musical instrument controlled without physical contact. The performer's hand position relative to two metal antennas controls frequency (pitch) and amplitude (volume).

How it works:

  • Right antenna (vertical): Controls pitch - move closer for higher notes
  • Left antenna (horizontal loop): Controls volume - move hand up to increase volume

This simulation uses 3D cursors as visual guides to control both parameters.

Electromagnetic Fields:

The visualization shows the capacitive fields around each antenna. These fields detect hand proximity and convert it into electrical signals:

  • Green field (pitch): Vertical ellipsoid around the straight antenna
  • Blue field (volume): Torus around the loop antenna
  • Particle density increases when cursors are being moved

User's Guide

Camera Controls:

  • Left Click + Drag: Rotate camera
  • Right Click + Drag: Pan camera
  • Scroll Wheel: Zoom in/out

Theremin Controls:

  • Click & Drag the green sphere (pitch cursor) up/down to change note frequency
  • Click & Drag the blue cylinder (volume cursor) up/down to adjust loudness
  • Audio starts automatically when you move the cursors

Audio Parameters

440 Hz (A4)
50%

Technical Info

Technology Stack:

  • BabylonJS 3D engine
  • Web Audio API (oscillator + gain)
  • Interactive 3D cursors

Prototype Status:

This is a technical prototype validating the feasibility of simulating a theremin in a web browser using 3D cursors for intuitive control.
