System

The Fire Triangle is the minimal complete model of combustion: three co-present conditions (Fuel, O₂, Heat) must simultaneously exceed their respective thresholds. Removing any one extinguishes the fire.

Ternary Structure

Fuel Combustible matter — cellulose, hydrocarbons… O₂ Oxidizer — atmospheric oxygen ≥16% Heat Activation energy — ignition temperature of fuel

Combustion Reaction

Fuel + O₂ + Heat → CO₂ + H₂O + ΔH Exothermic — the released ΔH sustains the reaction (positive feedback).

Extinction Methods

• Smother — displace O₂ below 16% (CO₂, foam, blanket) • Freeze — cool below ignition temperature (water, powder) • Remove Fuel — cut supply or create a fire break

TSCG Insight

Primary metaconcept: m2:Balance — the ternary synergy is a co-presence attractor: fire exists only as the intersection of three flows above three thresholds. Disrupting any one breaks the balance.
