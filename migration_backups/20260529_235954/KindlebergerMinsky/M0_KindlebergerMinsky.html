System

The Kindleberger-Minsky cycle is a recurrent 5-phase pattern in which a financial system transitions from stable equilibrium through speculative excess to systemic collapse. Driven by m2:Narration (collective belief propagation) and m2:Propagation (panic cascade).

Theoretical Basis

Hyman Minsky (1986) — Financial Instability Hypothesis: stability is destabilizing; firms evolve hedge → speculative → Ponzi.
Charles Kindleberger (1978) — 5-phase cycle, international contagion.
Robert Shiller (2019) — Narrative Economics, market narratives as causal forces.
Irving Fisher (1933) — Debt-deflation theory, Revulsion amplification.
Walter Bagehot (1873) — Lender of Last Resort (m2:Regulation).

Primary Pattern — m2:Propagation
S ⊗ I ⊗ A ⊗ D ⊗ F ⊗ V ⊗ Im
Panic spreads through the interbank network: each receiver becomes a new emitter (m2:Duplication). The Minsky Moment is the threshold attractor where R0 > 1. Copy fidelity V = panic signal integrity; Im = bilateral bank exposures.
Primary Pattern — m2:Narration
S ⊗ I ⊗ D ⊗ F ⊗ V ⊗ R ⊗ A ⊗ Im
Collective market belief morphism across 5 phases: "New paradigm" → "This time is different" → "Panic" → "Never again." The Collapse attractor (A) is structurally implicit at Euphoria peak. Topology = Linear. Shiller (2019): quantifiable via media analysis & surveys.
