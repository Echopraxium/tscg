System

A Trophic Pyramid is a layered ecological structure where each level dissipates ~90% of energy as heat (Lindeman 1942). This poclet adds Humanity as a supra-trophic super-predator (Level 6) and an Anthropic Pressure (AP) model grounded in The Limits to Growth (Meadows et al. 1972).

Lindeman's 10% Rule (1942)

η = E(n+1)/E(n) ≈ 0.10 10 000 → 1 000 → 100 → 10 kJ/m²/yr

6 Trophic Poles

Level 1 🌿 Producers — plants, phytoplankton (LEFT base) ⊥    🍄 Decomposers — fungi, bacteria (RIGHT base — transversal) Level 2 🐈 Herbivores — ungulates, zooplankton Level 3 🐱 Carnivores — foxes, predatory fish Level 4 🦅 Apex Predators — wolves, sharks Level 6 🌎 Humanity — supra-trophic super-predator

Anthropic Pressure (AP)

AP ∈ [0, 1] — fossil fuel exploitation + mining impact on biodiversity. Impact is top-down: Level 4 collapses at AP=0.33. AP_crit = 0.70 → irreversible cascade. Current 2024: AP ≈ 0.46

Decomposers — Split Base

The pyramid base is bisected vertically: Producers (LEFT box) | Decomposers (RIGHT box). Decomposers exhibit non-monotonic behaviour: irruption under moderate AP, collapse above AP = 0.60.

Humanity's Energy Bypass

E_human = Σ(f_n×E_n) + E_fossil Fossil input (~20 000 kJ/m²/yr) exceeds total natural primary production. The gold platform above the apex grows with AP.
