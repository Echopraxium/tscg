## Overview

TSCG (Transdisciplinary System Construction Game) is an **ontological framework** for analyzing and modeling complex systems across disciplines. It provides a rigorous mathematical foundation for understanding systemic patterns through a **bicephalous (two-headed) architecture** that combines:

- **Territory measurement** via Eagle Eye (ASFID dimensions)
- **Map construction** via Sphinx Eye (ORIVE dimensions)

The framework enables:
- Systematic analysis of complex systems across any domain
- Discovery of universal patterns (metaconcepts) that transcend disciplines
- Validation of theoretical frameworks through minimal complete examples (poclets)
- Bridging between empirical observation and conceptual modeling

---

## Core Philosophy

### Map-Territory Distinction

TSCG is grounded in Alfred Korzybski's principle: **"The map is not the territory"**

- **Territory** (Eagle Eye/ASFID): Observable reality, measured phenomena, what actually happens
- **Map** (Sphinx Eye/ORIVE): Conceptual models, representations, how we understand and communicate

**Key Insight**: Both perspectives are necessary. Territory provides empirical grounding; Map enables understanding and communication. The epistemic gap between them (‚àÜŒò) is not a failure‚Äîit's an inherent feature of knowledge construction.

### Three Pillars

1. **Mathematical Rigor**: All concepts formalized through Hilbert spaces and tensor products
2. **Empirical Validation**: Theory constantly tested against real-world poclets
3. **Philosophical Coherence**: Maintains balance between realism and constructivism

## Getting Started

### Prerequisites

- Basic understanding of systems thinking
- Familiarity with linear algebra (vectors, tensors) helpful but not required
- Interest in transdisciplinary analysis

### Quick Start: Analyzing a System

1. **Measure Territory (ASFID)**:
   - A: What are the attractors/equilibria?
   - S: What is the structural organization?
   - F: What flows exist with environment? (F‚â•0.1)
   - I: What is the information complexity?
   - D: What is the rate of change?

2. **Construct Map (ORIVE)** (optional):
   - O: How observable is this system?
   - R: How well can we represent it?
   - I: How shareable/interoperable is the model?
   - V: How verifiable/testable is it?
   - E: How evolvable/updatable is the model?

3. **Identify Metaconcepts**:
   - Check the 60 M2 metaconcepts
   - Which patterns are present?
   - Note polarity (neutral, dual, N-ary, hybrid)

4. **Assign Domain**:
   - Which M1 extension fits?
   - Biology, Chemistry, Optics, Photography, Mythology?
   - Or does it need new extension?

5. **Evaluate as Poclet**:
   - Is it minimal yet complete?
   - All ASFID dimensions present?
   - Serves as proof-of-concept?
