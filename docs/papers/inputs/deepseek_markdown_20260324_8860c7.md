## 🌍 Why This Document?

This README articulates the **fundamental vocation** of the TSCG project, as it emerged from a reflection on its deepest nature.

TSCG is not yet another “universal model” claiming to hold the key to everything. It is a **language infrastructure**, a **bridge** between disciplines, a **translation tool** that allows experts from different fields to finally collaborate without getting lost in their respective jargons.

**Two words define this vocation: *debabelization* and *desiloification*.**

---

## 🗼 Debabelization: Dismantling the Tower of Babel

The Tower of Babel is not merely about the multiplicity of languages. It is about the **impossibility of cooperating** because we no longer understand each other.

In science and knowledge, we live in a Babelian situation:

| Domain | Speaks of… |
|--------|------------|
| **Biologist** | “Homeostasis” |
| **Sociologist** | “Social regulation” |
| **Engineer** | “Feedback loop” |
| **Physician** | “Maintaining physiological balance” |

These terms refer to **isomorphic systemic realities**. Yet without a common language, each remains in their silo, believing their concept is unique to their field. Academic **glottophagy**—each discipline inventing its own vocabulary to assert legitimacy—only deepens the divides.

**TSCG provides a pivot language** (M2, the 68 metaconcepts) that makes it possible to say:

> *“What you call ‘homeostasis’ in biology, and what I call ‘negative feedback regulation’ in control theory, is the same structure: **A⊗S⊗F** in ℂ¹⁰ space. Let’s talk about it.”*

This is not a universal model. It is a **translator**, a **bridge**.

---

## 🏭 Desiloification: Breaking Down Silos

The silo is that vertical, enclosed tower where grain is stored without being shared. The **siloification of knowledge** is the paradoxical movement where, the more we know, the less we can communicate that knowledge to those who don’t share exactly the same framework.

The consequences are severe:

- **Inability to address complex problems** (climate, global health, transitions) that are by nature **cross-silo**
- **Absurd duplication** of concepts: each field reinvents what others have already formalized
- **Prestige hierarchies**: some silos consider themselves more “fundamental” than others

TSCG does not add another silo (“general systems theory”). It proposes a **horizontal infrastructure** that enables:

- Making concepts **circulate** between silos
- **Identifying isomorphisms** (what is structurally identical despite different names)
- **Validating robustness** by confronting a model with other domains (a “poclet” that works in biology *and* chemistry *and* mythology has something structurally interesting)

## 🧭 The Epistemological Gesture

By choosing these terms—*debabelization*, *desiloification*—you enact a radical shift:

| Traditional Ambition | TSCG’s Gesture |
|----------------------|----------------|
| Produce a total model | Provide a **common language** |
| Prove a universal truth | Enable **faithful translations** between domains |
| Impose itself as a queen discipline | **Serve as infrastructure** for disciplines |
| Close the debate | **Open the circulation** of concepts |
| Be a “grand narrative” | Be a **tool for shared intelligibility** |

This is a stance of profound **epistemological humility**: TSCG does not claim to know what the world is. It only claims to offer a **language sufficiently regular and rich** for those who seek to understand the world to finally **cooperate without losing themselves in words**.

The debabelization/desiloification vocation is not an afterthought—it is woven into the very structure of TSCG:

| Component | Role in Debabelization |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **M3 (ℂ¹⁰)** | The **space of all possible maps and territories**—where translation becomes possible |
| **M2 (68 metaconcepts)** | The **universal pattern library**—the Esperanto vocabulary |
| **M1 extensions** | **Domain dialects**—how the common language adapts to specific fields |
| **M0 poclets** | **Validated translations**—proof that the bridge works across disciplines |
| **∆Θ (epistemic gap)** | The **translation error**—what we measure to refine our understanding |

Just as Esperanto provides a regular, neutral language for linguistic translation, TSCG provides a **formal, neutral language for conceptual translation** between knowledge fields.
