## 1. The Stereoscopic Glasses Metaphor

Anaglyphic stereoscopic glasses — one red lens, one blue lens — do not
change the scene they observe. They do not prove that the scene is
interesting. They do not criticise its composition. They simply add one
dimension of reading that the naked eye cannot access: **depth**.

The depth was always there. The glasses reveal it.

```
Left lens  (red)   =  🦅 Eagle Eye / ASFID / Territory
                       "What is the system doing empirically?"

Right lens (blue)  =  🦁 Sphinx Eye / REVOI / Map
                       "How well can we represent it?"

Stereoscopic image =  🔭 Gs / Stereopsis / δ₁
                       "What depth emerges from combining both views?"
```

This metaphor is not external to TSCG — it is TSCG's own internal
structure made visible. **Gs (Stereopsis) is already formalised in
M3_GenesisGrammar** as the reification of the Eagle Eye / Sphinx Eye
synergy. The glasses are not a communication device added afterwards:
they are what TSCG structurally *is*.

## 2. The Operational Vocation: Reformulation

### What TSCG Does NOT Do

- Does **not** prove that a theory is correct
- Does **not** demonstrate that a model is better than another
- Does **not** criticise the internal logic of a system
- Does **not** replace domain expertise
- Does **not** claim to be the only valid reading

### What TSCG Does

**It reformulates** — any system, theory, model, myth, or phenomenon —
in the Systemic Esperanto vocabulary, producing a structural reading
that reveals patterns invisible to domain-internal observation.

The reformulation is:
- **Non-destructive**: the original system is not modified or invalidated
- **Additive**: a new dimension of reading is added, not substituted
- **Reversible**: the glasses can be removed; the original reading remains
- **Scalable**: applicable to any system that can be described structurally

### The Posture in Practice

```
Input  :  Any system, theory, model, myth, phenomenon

Process:  Apply ASFID/REVOI scoring
          Identify M2 GenericConcepts
          Compute δ₁ (epistemic gap)
          Assign SpectralClass

Output :  A structural reading — not a verdict
          "This system, seen through TSCG glasses, looks like this."
```

The output is always a *reading*, never a *judgment*.

## 3. The Three Pillars as One Pair of Glasses

The three core hypotheses of TSCG — which appear as separate architectural
layers — are in fact three aspects of the same stereoscopic gesture:

### The Red Lens: Territory/Map Dichotomy

The left lens (Eagle Eye/ASFID) measures what the system *does* in the
Territory. The right lens (Sphinx Eye/REVOI) evaluates how well our Map
*represents* it. The gap between them (δ₁) is the depth signal — the
structural information that neither lens alone can produce.

Without the dichotomy, there are no glasses — just one eye, no depth.

### The Frame: Systemic Esperanto

The frame that holds both lenses is the M2 vocabulary — the ~75
GenericConcepts that provide a common language for describing what both
lenses see. Without a shared vocabulary, the two views cannot be combined
into a single stereoscopic image.

The Esperanto does not impose a language on the system — it provides a
*translation medium* that makes the two views commensurable.

### The Models: LEGO Technic

The stereoscopic image produced by the glasses is not the system itself —
it is a **structural model** of the system, built from compositional
components (M2 GenericConcepts) according to formal assembly rules
(Monoidal Grammar). It is a maquette, not a replica.

```
A LEGO Technic model of the Eiffel Tower:
- Is not the Eiffel Tower
- Does not replace the Eiffel Tower
- Does not criticise the Eiffel Tower's engineering
- Captures the Tower's structural principles at a different scale
- Is consciously and deliberately simplified
- Is useful precisely because of its simplification
```

A TSCG poclet is to its source system what a LEGO model is to its
subject: a consciously reduced, structurally informative representation
that reveals patterns by deliberately leaving out details.

## 5. The Eiffel Tower Maquette Principle

The maquette metaphor is the most important guard against both Narcissus
and Icarus:

**Against Narcissus (self-validation)**:
A maquette builder knows the maquette is not the Tower. The structural
reading produced by TSCG is not the system — it is a model of the system,
at a different scale, through a specific set of lenses. Confusing the
model with the reality is the Narcissus error.

**Against Icarus (excessive claims)**:
A maquette does not claim to be architecturally superior to the original.
It claims to be *useful for understanding structural principles* that the
full-scale original makes hard to see. TSCG does not claim to explain
systems better than domain experts — it claims to reveal structural
patterns that domain-internal observation misses.

```
"This is a maquette. It is small. It is simplified.
 It is not the Tower. And it is useful precisely for these reasons."
```

## 6. The "For Dummies" Corollary

An occasional self-description of TSCG:

> *"A Systemic Modelling Toolkit for Dummies"*

This is not false modesty. It is **epistemic precision** about the intended
user and the intended use:

- **For Dummies** = accessible without prior specialisation in systems theory
- **Systemic Modelling** = produces structural models, not proofs
- **Toolkit** = a set of instruments, not a theory to be believed

The paradox: a toolkit that calls itself "for dummies" and produces
structural readings recognised by nuclear engineers and blockchain
researchers is not, in practice, trivial. The simplicity of the interface
does not imply simplicity of the reading it enables.

This is the LEGO principle applied to epistemology: the brick is simple;
what you can build with it is not.

## 7. What Success Looks Like

Given this operational vocation, success for TSCG is precisely defined:

> **TSCG succeeds if it enables a new structural reading for engineers
> and researchers doing technology watch — even without being formally
> adopted in their projects.**

A reader who, after encountering TSCG, finds themselves spontaneously
asking *"what is the Attractor of this system?"* or *"what is the
epistemic gap between how this field models itself and what it actually
does?"* — that reader has successfully put on the glasses.

They may never use a poclet. They may never read the SHACL grammar.
They may never score an instance. And yet the glasses are working.

```
Level 1 — "Interesting"          : glasses tried on, then set down
Level 2 — "New reading"          ← success criterion
           glasses worn occasionally, structural questions asked
Level 3 — "Working tool"         : glasses worn habitually
Level 4 — "Community standard"   : glasses distributed and shared
```

Level 2 is already a genuine cognitive transformation. It is also the
most honest estimate of what a 5-month-old, 1-cook, 24-poclet toolkit
can realistically achieve in the near term.
