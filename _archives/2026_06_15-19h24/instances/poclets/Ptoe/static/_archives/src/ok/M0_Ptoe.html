Element Families

Alkali Metals

Highly reactive metals in Group 1 with one valence electron. They form +1 cations and react vigorously with water. Examples: Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Fr.

Alkaline Earth Metals

Reactive metals in Group 2 with two valence electrons. They form +2 cations and are less reactive than alkali metals. Examples: Be, Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba, Ra.

Transition Metals

Metals in Groups 3-12 with variable oxidation states. They have partially filled d-orbitals and often form colored compounds. Examples: Fe, Cu, Zn, Ag, Au, Pt.

Post-Transition Metals

Metals in Groups 13-15 that are softer and have lower melting points than transition metals. Examples: Al, Ga, In, Sn, Tl, Pb, Bi.

Metalloids

Elements with properties intermediate between metals and nonmetals. They are semiconductors. Examples: B, Si, Ge, As, Sb, Te.

Nonmetals

Elements that gain electrons in reactions to form anions. They form covalent bonds and are essential for life. Examples: C, N, O, P, S, Se.

Halogens

Highly reactive nonmetals in Group 17 with seven valence electrons. They form -1 anions and diatomic molecules. Examples: F, Cl, Br, I, At.

Noble Gases

Inert gases in Group 18 with full valence shells. They rarely form compounds due to their stability. Examples: He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn.

Lanthanides

Rare earth metals in the f-block (atomic numbers 57-71). They have similar chemical properties and are used in magnets and electronics. Examples: Ce, Nd, Eu, Gd, Tb.

Actinides

Radioactive elements in the f-block (atomic numbers 89-103). Many are synthetic and unstable. They include nuclear fuels. Examples: Th, U, Np, Pu, Am, Cm.
