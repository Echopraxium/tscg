Ideal Gas Law

pV = nRT  — direct governing equation for the gas phase. p = pressure (Pa), V = volume (m³), n = mol, R = 8.314 J·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹, T = temperature (K).

Gibbs Phase Rule

F = C − P + 2 For a pure substance (C=1):  1 phase → F=2 (T and P free)  ·  2-phase coexistence → F=1 (boundary curve)  ·  triple point → F=0 (invariant).

Clausius–Clapeyron

ln(P₂/P₁) = ΔHvap/R × (1/T₁ − 1/T₂) Used to compute Tb(P) from the reference boiling point at 1 atm.

Phase Animations

• Solid — 48 particles on a grid vibrating thermally around rest positions. Amplitude ∝ T/Tm. • Liquid — 70 particles in an analytically divergence-free stream-function velocity field. Transient bonds form when d < 35 px. • Gas — 22 particles following Langevin–Brownian dynamics (random kicks + damping). Elastic wall reflections.
