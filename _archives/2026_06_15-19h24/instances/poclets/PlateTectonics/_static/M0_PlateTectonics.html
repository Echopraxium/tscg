Finite Element Simulation of Plate Tectonics – Realistic Deformations

Overview

This simulation uses a simplified finite‑element approach (stress diffusion) to model lithosphere‑mantle interaction.

Deformation by Layer

1. Plate (Lithosphere)

Elastic – Flexure at ridges, subduction zones, topographic loading. Small strains (10⁻⁶–10⁻⁴). Plastic (brittle) – Faulting in the upper brittle part (0–15 km crust, up to 40 km cold mantle). Viscous (ductile) – Dislocation creep below ~600–700 °C (lower lithosphere).

2. Mantle (Asthenosphere + Lower Mantle)

Dominant regime – Non‑Newtonian viscous flow (power‑law creep, n ≈ 3). Realistic strain types – Simple shear (basal drag), Pure shear (divergence/convergence), Rigid‑body rotation. Volume change – Negligible → enforce incompressibility (∇·v=0). Strain rates – 10⁻¹⁵ – 10⁻¹² s⁻¹.

3. Magma (Partial Melt Zones)

Localized pockets – Ridges, plumes, subduction zones. Rheology – Newtonian viscous (η ∼ 10¹–10⁴ Pa·s).

Simulation Controls

Convection – intensity of mantle circulation. Slab density – weight of subducting plate (slab pull). Asymmetry – preferred direction of plate motion. Propagation – stress diffusion coefficient. Radius – influence distance for neighbour coupling. Quake Threshold – stress level needed to trigger an earthquake (30%-95%).

Interaction

Click on a mantle cell – injects a stress perturbation (mantle plume or magmatic intrusion). Propagate Stress button – forces diffusion of stress to neighboring cells. Stress then diffuses according to a non‑linear viscosity law (n=3). Cell colour and scale reflect stress level. Earthquake triggers when average stress > threshold.

Planet Mode

Click on the grid – select a meridian/parallel cell. Sample button – extract geological data at that location.

Visual Feedback

Cell colour – from orange (low stress) to red (high stress). Cell scaling – expands with stress. Fault lines – colour changes with global stress. Earthquake – camera shake and domino effect on buildings.

Technical Notes

Grid – 16×3×4 prismatic cells (doubled in length). Stress diffusion – non‑linear, exponent n=3. Forcing – slab pull (right side) + ridge push (center) + basal drag.

Reference

Turcotte, D.L. & Schubert, G. (2014) – Geodynamics, 3rd edition. Cambridge University Press.
