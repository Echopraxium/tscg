## 4. CRITICAL BIOLOGICAL DISTINCTIONS

### 4.1 Chrysalis ≠ Cocoon
```
COMMON MISCONCEPTION CLARIFICATION:
───────────────────────────────────

❌ WRONG: "Butterfly exits cocoon"
✓ CORRECT: "Butterfly emerges from chrysalis"

Chrysalis: Hardened pupal CASE (exoskeleton)
Cocoon:    Silk WRAPPING around chrysalis (moths, not butterflies)
```

### 4.2 Emergence (Pole 5) - Why It Matters
```
EMERGENCE AS DISTINCT POLE
──────────────────────────

Traditional 5-Pole Model:  Egg → Larva → Pupa → Adult

Enhanced 6-Pole Model:     Egg → Larva → Pre-Pupa → Pupa → EMERGENCE → Adult
                                                              ▲

                                              Captures wing deployment,
                                              max predation risk,
                                              hemolymph hydraulics
```

**Why Emergence Deserves Its Own Pole**:
- **Fastest Dynamics**: Maximum dD/dt (2-4 hours of explosive change)
- **Peak Vulnerability**: Cannot fly, soft cuticle, predictable timing
- **Unique Physiology**: Hemolymph pumping (up to 15 kPa), wing deployment
