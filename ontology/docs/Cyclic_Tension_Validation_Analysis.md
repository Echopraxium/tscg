## Definition Recap

**Cyclic Tension**: Perpetual alternation between opposing processes (destruction ↔ restoration, catabolism ↔ anabolism) maintaining system viability through dynamic equilibrium. The tension/stress is **constitutive** (normal operating mode), not pathological. System requires both poles - removing either → collapse.

**Key Properties**:
- **Perpetuity**: No final resolution, continues indefinitely
- **Opposition**: Two opposed processes (degradation vs regeneration)
- **Constitutive Stress**: Tension is essential to function, not dysfunction
- **Dynamic Equilibrium**: System stable *because* of alternation, not despite it

## Poclet 3: Four-Stroke Engine

### Searching for Cyclic Tension...

#### Candidate 1: Combustion ↔ Cooling
- **Degradation**: Power stroke generates heat (up to 2500°C) → thermal stress
- **Restoration**: Cooling system removes heat → prevents engine damage
- **Cycle**: Continuous during operation

**Formula Check**:
- D (Dynamics): ✅ Combustion events, heat dissipation
- A (Attractor): ✅ Optimal operating temperature (90-105°C)
- F (Flow): ✅ Heat generation flow, coolant flow

**Constitutive?**: ✅ YES - Combustion generates power (required); cooling prevents meltdown (required). Removing either → engine failure.

#### Candidate 2: Carbon Buildup ↔ Combustion Cleaning
- **Degradation**: Incomplete combustion → carbon deposits on valves/pistons
- **Restoration**: High-temperature combustion burns off deposits ("Italian tune-up")
- **Cycle**: Continuous low-speed ↔ high-speed operation

**Formula Check**:
- D (Dynamics): ✅ Deposit accumulation, burn-off dynamics
- A (Attractor): ✅ Clean engine state (optimal compression)
- F (Flow): ✅ Carbon deposition flux, combustion cleaning flux

**Constitutive?**: ⚠️ PARTIAL - Modern engines minimize deposits via fuel additives. Not strictly essential but present in practice.

#### Candidate 3: Piston Ring Wear ↔ Lubrication Film Renewal
- **Degradation**: Piston-cylinder friction wears rings/cylinder walls
- **Restoration**: Oil film renewal prevents metal-on-metal contact
- **Cycle**: Every piston stroke (intake stroke spreads oil)

**Formula Check**:
- D (Dynamics): ✅ Wear dynamics, lubrication renewal
- A (Attractor): ✅ Minimal friction/wear equilibrium
- F (Flow): ✅ Material wear flux, oil circulation flux

**Constitutive?**: ✅ YES - Wear is inevitable (friction required for sealing); lubrication prevents catastrophic wear. Both poles define engine longevity.

### Validation Score: **9/10** ✅ STRONGLY VALIDATED
- Three manifestations found
- Formula D⊗A⊗F applies perfectly (candidates 1 & 3)
- Constitutive nature clear (combustion-cooling, wear-lubrication essential)
- Four-Stroke demonstrates Cyclic Tension in thermodynamic/mechanical domain

**Four-Stroke Engine confirms Cyclic Tension in engineering systems**
