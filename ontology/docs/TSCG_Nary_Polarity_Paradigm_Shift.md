## Historical Context

### v1.0 - v12.0: Binary Restriction Era

The TSCG framework initially adopted a **binary polarity principle**:

```
"m2:polarityPrinciple": "Binary only: neutral (unique) or dual (intrinsically 
bidirectional). N-ary polarity rejected."
```

**Rationale** (implicit):
- Simplicity: Binary classification is mathematically elegant
- Dualism tradition: Many philosophical systems are dualistic (yin/yang, good/evil)
- Tensor products: Dual polarity captures bidirectional metaconcepts naturally

**Contradiction discovered**:
- **Switch** metaconcept (v12.0.0) was already explicitly N-ary: "Binary (N=2) is special case; general Switch is N-ary"
- **Mode** metaconcept (v12.0.0) described N-ary operational states
- Framework claimed to reject N-ary but already contained N-ary metaconcepts

### v13.0: N-ary Generalization Era

The **Magic Color Wheel poclet** analysis revealed that:
1. Real philosophical systems are often **pentagonal** (N=5) or **multipolar** (N>5)
2. Binary restriction was **arbitrary**, not empirically grounded
3. **Switch** and **Mode** already demonstrated N-ary worked in practice

**Decision**: Adopt N-ary polarity framework, making binary (N=2) a **special case**, not the exclusive option.

- **Advantage**: Theoretically coherent, empirically grounded, enables future poclets

## Philosophical Implications

### Why Binary Thinking Dominated (Historically)

1. **Cognitive simplicity**: Binary categories are easiest to process
2. **Logic tradition**: Boolean logic, law of excluded middle
3. **Dualist traditions**: Yin/Yang, Good/Evil, Mind/Body
4. **Computational influence**: Digital computers are binary (0/1)

### Why N-ary Is More Realistic

1. **Value pluralism**: Real ethics involves multiple incommensurable values
2. **Political complexity**: Left/Right is oversimplification (compass needs 2+ axes)
3. **Psychological richness**: Personality isn't single spectrum (Big Five needs N=5)
4. **Cultural diversity**: Different cultures use different N (Western dualism vs Chinese quintuplicity)

### The "Pentagonal Mystery"

Why does N=5 appear so often?
- **Cognitive capacity**: 5±2 items in working memory (Miller's Law)
- **Balance**: Rich enough for nuance, simple enough to grasp
- **Geometry**: Pentagon has unique properties (golden ratio, quasi-symmetry)
- **Cultural convergence**: Independent discovery across civilizations suggests deep pattern
