## 📐 Two Reform Phases

### Phase 1 (2026-05-11) — Symbolic reform
```
⊗  was kept but redefined:
   "structural co-activation operator" (monoidal product)
   NOT "tensor product in a Hilbert space"
```

### Phase 2 (2026-05-18) — Notation reform
```
⊗ replaced by three distinct operators:

×  →  Territory structural product  (intra-Gt, closed within Gt)
+  →  Map structural sum             (intra-Gm, closed within Gm)
|  →  Stereopsic fusion              (inter-grammar → always Gs)
```

## 🔑 Intra vs Inter-grammar Operators

```
× and +  →  intra-grammar (closed — stay within their grammar)
|        →  inter-grammar (open — traverses Gt/Gm → always produces Gs)
```

**Important:** This asymmetry is explained entirely within the Lambek
calculus / free monoidal grammar framework.
**No tensor algebra. No multi-sorted algebra. No structure beyond free monoids.**

## 🧬 The Cell Membrane Analogy for Operator |

### Why | has "extended rights"

The inter-grammar nature of | — its ability to traverse the Territory/Map
boundary while × and + remain closed — is illuminated by the analogy of a
**cell membrane**:

```
Territory Gt  ≈  interior of cell (cytoplasm)
Map Gm        ≈  exterior of cell (environment)
Operator |    ≈  cell membrane — the active interface
Gs primitives ≈  surface proteins and ion channels of the membrane
```

The cell membrane is not merely a barrier — it is an **active interface**
with its own functionalities (ion channels, surface proteins, pumps).
Analogously, | is not merely a crossing point — it generates its own
types of Gs (T, future primitives).
