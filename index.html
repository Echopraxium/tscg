System

A Trophic Pyramid is a layered hierarchical structure in which each level consumes biomass from below and dissipates ~90% of received energy as heat. This thermodynamic constraint — Lindeman's 10% rule — produces the universal pyramidal biomass shape: StratifiedDissipation.

Lindeman's 10% Rule (1942)

η = E(n+1) / E(n) ≈ 0.10 Energy cascade: 10 000 → 1 000 → 100 → 10 kJ/m²/yr Validated across terrestrial, freshwater and marine ecosystems.

5 Trophic Poles

Level 1  Primary Producers — plants, phytoplankton Level 2  Herbivores — insects, ungulates, zooplankton Level 3  Carnivores — foxes, snakes, predatory fish Level 4  Apex Predators — wolves, sharks, eagles ⊥      Decomposers — fungi, bacteria (cross-cutting)

Key GenericConcepts

• m2:Layer (S⊗I⊗A⊗R) — each level is a stable positional stratum • m2:Dissipation (F⊗D) — irreversible thermodynamic loss per boundary • m2:Context (O⊗R⊗Im⊗E) — biome determines species composition

Trophic Cascade

Click “Remove Apex Predator” to trigger: Apex removed → Carnivores irrupt → Herbivores collapse → Producers rebound. Famous case: wolf reintroduction in Yellowstone (1995) changed river morphology.
